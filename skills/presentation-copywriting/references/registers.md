## 5.1 The benefit–feature ladder sets the altitude [MP §3]

Ladder: attribute/feature (verifiable fact) → advantage (vs the alternative) → functional benefit (time/cost/risk/accuracy delta) → emotional/identity benefit. Connector test: each rung must survive "so what?" asked by the ACTUAL audience — and pressure-test DOWNWARD: every emotional claim traces to a verifiable attribute or it reads as a leap. Altitude is chosen per audience, not maximized; one rung per slide. Mechanically appending "…to save time and money" to every feature is template output the buyer recognizes [MP §3.3].

## 5.2 The dial map

| Copy dial | Executive | Presales / technical | Marketing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Benefit altitude | business case only (money/time/risk, quantified) | feature + mechanism; functional benefit stated once, quantified, unemotionally | functional→emotional, 1 proof anchor per promise (design §8.3) |
| Headline payload | decision + quantified so-what | mechanism + measured result | benefit or vivid outcome |
| Who states the benefit | writer states outcome; mechanism → appendix | writer states mechanism; READER derives benefit | writer states benefit; feature is the proof |
| Precision | pre-computed deltas vs goal | exact numbers + baselines + conditions | concrete scene + 1 exact number |
| Hype vocabulary (§8 group A) | zero | zero | budget ≤1 deliberate use |
| Hedging | hedge estimates (ranges), never the ask | attributed bounds ON the slide; limitations line on every benchmark slide | footnote-level, still specific |
| Curiosity titles | 0 | ~0 (answer-first; peers test claims) | ≤2/deck at hook beats |
| Proof genre | peer-company named outcome | demo / reproducible benchmark / repro steps | named customer + number |
| Damaging admission | risks-with-mitigations slide | explicit trade-offs + not-for list | soft ("not for everyone") |
| JTBD device | outcomes rolled to KPI language | outcome statements at value beats | job story as the hook |
| CTA | the real ask + date + owner | reproducible next step on THEIR data | low-friction; first-person only if clickable |
| Pronoun stance | "we" (unity) + named owners | "it/we-did" factual; sparse "you" | "you/your" dominant |

## 5.3 Worked mechanics — rendering one locked fact three ways

Take any fact-bank tuple of the general shape {measured delta, unit, aggregation, baseline, test conditions, version, date, hedge}. Produce the three renderings with the SAME procedure and three different parameter sets — never with three separate drafting acts:

**Step 1 — rung selection (stop rules A–D, translation-procedure §6.1).**
- Executive → business-outcome rung, but ONLY as far as a fact-bank assumption chain supports; if the outcome rung requires an unverified assumption, the title descends one rung and the assumption is flagged.
- Presales → mechanism + functional-benefit rung; the benefit stated once, quantified, unemotionally.
- Marketing → one rung above functional (identity/emotion permitted) IF the rung below is visible on the slide or one proof anchor is adjacent (design §8.3).

**Step 2 — payload construction (title-craft §1.6).**
- Executive: the delta pre-computed against a goal or baseline the exec owns; the decision it enables is the grammatical payload.
- Presales: the NAMED mechanism is the subject; the measured result is the payload (mechanism-naming beats benefit-claiming — engineers evaluate by simulating the mechanism; the Hopkins preemptive-claim move rediscovered by devtools marketing [MP §6.1]).
- Marketing: the audience's outcome is the payload, in their workflow vocabulary; the vendor appears as agent only.

**Step 3 — precision routing (title-craft §1.5; translation-procedure §6.3).**
- Executive: title rounds (≤2–3 sig figs); exact figure to notes backup with scent.
- Presales: source precision + baseline + conditions ON the slide (subtitle or label type); no naked deltas.
- Marketing: one exact number kept as the credibility anchor; the scene stays concrete; everything else rounds.

**Step 4 — hedge form (translation-procedure §6.4).**
- Executive: estimates as numeric ranges; the ask itself never hedged.
- Presales: an attributed, bounded limitations line ON the benchmark slide — owner named, scope named, boundary named (Jensen 2008: bounded, attributed limitations INCREASE trustworthiness of scientist AND communicator; replicated 2011 — an inverted small-model summary of this paper was caught and discarded during research).
- Marketing: hedges live in footnote/micro copy, still specific, never vague.

**Step 5 — proof attachment (body-microcopy §2.4)** per the register's proof-genre ladder, co-located with the claim.

Invariant across all three renderings: the tuple (SKILL.md prime directive) survives verbatim in meaning; only altitude, vocabulary, precision PLACEMENT, and hedge FORM change. The fact-fidelity §7.1 diff must map all three back to the same source tuple. Never average registers — "mechanism + hype benefit" reads worst of all combinations [MP §6.1].

## 5.4 Technical-credibility specifics (presales register) [MP §6]

- Technical audiences pre-disbelieve vendor content (direction HIGH, exact figures LOW); copy for them is trust-repair, not persuasion.
- **The damaging admission** (Sugarman): voluntarily state a real, costly drawback early — cost, setup effort, who it's NOT for — then bound or resolve it. It inoculates and makes adjacent claims believable. It must be genuinely costly: a humble-brag admission burns more trust than silence. Converges with structure F3 (a limitations slide increases credibility) and F4 steelman.
- **Denominator discipline**: never "significantly faster", never a naked delta — every %/multiple names its baseline and conditions; an engineer who can't reconstruct the denominator discounts the numerator [MP §6.5; TT §5.2].

## 5.5 Mixed rooms

When one deck serves multiple registers (the common exec-plus-evaluators meeting): write titles and claims at the DECISION-MAKER's register (jargon decided for the least-expert person with decision power [TT §4.7]); serve the other reader through the stack's lower layers — mechanism and conditions in subtitle/label type for the evaluator under an exec-register title, or an appendix pointer for the exec under a presales-register body. Never alternate registers slide-by-slide (register collision, anti-patterns §8-27); layer within the copy stack instead. Structure F3's layering (big picture → strategic detail → deep dive) is the ordering-level twin.
