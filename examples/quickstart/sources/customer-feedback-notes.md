# Customer Interview Notes — Q2 (internal)

**Conducted by:** Customer Success, Northlight Insights
**Method:** 30-minute structured calls, 3 accounts selected to span small/mid/enterprise tier
**Distribution:** Product and Customer Success leadership only

## Interview 1 — Harlow Freight (mid-market, 32 seats, customer since Q3 last year)

Contact: Ops Manager (day-to-day admin of the account).

- "The dashboard used to just spin. Now it loads before I've let go of the mouse. That's the
  difference between my team using this every morning and using it once a week."
- Pain point: no way to get notified when a metric crosses a threshold — currently checks
  dashboards manually every morning "just in case something moved overnight."
- Expanded seat count from 24 to 32 in Q2, cited the dashboard-builder feature as the reason:
  "we stopped needing to ask your team for one-off reports."

## Interview 2 — Corbel Systems (enterprise, 140 seats, customer since Q1 two years ago)

Contact: Director of Analytics.

- "You fixed the thing that was actually costing us — the latency, not some feature we'd never
  asked for. That's rare."
- Pain point (raised unprompted): "We evaluated [competitor] this quarter mostly because they
  have real-time alerts and you don't. We're staying with you, but that gap is real and it's
  the first thing I'd ask for."
- Confirmed renewal at current tier; declined to expand further this cycle specifically because
  the alerting gap blocks a planned use case (fraud-monitoring dashboards for their ops team).

## Interview 3 — Ferro & Vance (small, 8 seats, customer since Q2 this year)

Contact: Founder / primary user.

- "I built my own dashboard in twenty minutes without asking anyone. I didn't expect that from
  a tool this size."
- Pain point: pricing tiers unclear when approaching the 10-seat threshold — asked CS to clarify
  before expanding further. (Flagged to Sales; not a product issue.)
- Has not yet hit a case for real-time alerting given team size, but asked if it was "on the
  roadmap" when told about Corbel's use case in passing.

## Cross-account theme

Two of three accounts (Corbel, Harlow Freight) raised the absence of real-time/threshold
alerting unprompted, independent of each other. Both cited it as the main open gap relative to
otherwise-positive sentiment. No account raised dashboard latency as a current problem — all
references to it were in the past tense, crediting the Q2 fix.
