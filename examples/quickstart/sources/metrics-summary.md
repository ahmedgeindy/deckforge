# Northlight Insights — Q2 Metrics Summary (internal)

**Prepared by:** RevOps, Northlight Insights
**Period:** Q2 (Apr–Jun), compared against Q1 and against Q2 of the prior year
**Distribution:** Product, Sales, and Customer Success leadership only

## Revenue

- Net new ARR: $1.42M this quarter, up from $0.97M in Q1 (+46% quarter-over-quarter).
- Expansion revenue (upsell + seat growth on existing accounts): $610K this quarter, vs. $340K
  in Q1. Expansion is now 43% of net new ARR, up from 35% in Q1.
- Headcount on the Northlight Insights team: unchanged at 14 (11 eng, 2 product, 1 design) since
  Q1 — the revenue growth above did not come from adding people.

## Retention

- Gross revenue churn: 2.1% this quarter, down from 3.4% in Q1 and 4.0% in the same quarter last
  year.
- Net revenue retention: 108%, up from 101% in Q1. This is the second consecutive quarter of
  improvement.
- Logo churn: 3 accounts lost this quarter (out of 214 total), all three under $15K ACV.

## Customer sentiment

- NPS: 41, up from 29 in Q1. Survey population: 86 respondents (up from 61 in Q1), response rate
  34%.
- Support ticket volume per active account: down 18% quarter-over-quarter, attributed to the
  Q2 dashboard-latency fix (see milestone 2 below).

## Product milestones shipped in Q2

1. **Self-serve dashboard builder** (shipped week 4) — customers can now compose custom
   dashboards without filing a request with the Northlight team. Adoption: 61% of accounts with
   >5 seats have built at least one custom dashboard as of end of Q2.
2. **Dashboard latency fix** — median dashboard load time reduced from 4.1s to 1.3s. Directly
   cited in 2 of the 3 customer interviews this quarter (see customer-feedback-notes.md).
3. **SOC 2 Type II renewal** — completed on schedule, no findings. Table-stakes for the two
   enterprise deals currently in procurement.

## Known gap

- Real-time alerting (threshold-based notifications on live metrics) is not yet built. This is
  the most frequently requested feature in the Q2 sales-loss debrief (cited in 6 of 14 lost
  competitive deals) and in customer interviews (see customer-feedback-notes.md).
