# Presentation Brief — Northlight Insights Q2 Business Review

**Requested by:** Dana Ruiz, VP Product, Northlight Insights
**Deck owner:** Product & Customer Success leadership
**Deadline:** Draft ready for internal review 3 business days before the meeting

## Audience

Executive staff meeting (quarterly business review): CEO, CFO, VP Sales, VP Customer Success,
and the board observer seat. Six people, all senior, all time-poor. Stance: generally
supportive of the product team but the CFO flagged in the last cycle that continued tooling
spend needs to show a clearer return. Room is warm, not hostile — nobody is trying to kill the
product, but the budget line is not a rubber stamp this time. Assume a skeptical-on-ROI,
aligned-on-mission audience.

## Goal

**Decide.** The deck must get explicit approval to renew the Q3 budget for the Northlight
Insights product team (headcount + infra spend) at the current run rate. This is a decision
meeting, not a status update — the ask is on the table by the second slide.

## Format

20-minute slot inside the quarterly business review. Live, in person, one continuous session
(no breakout). Slides will be presented from a laptop; expect interruption and cross-talk from
the CFO in particular — design so any slide can be the last slide if the meeting runs short.

## Artifact

Presented live. A leave-behind version (same file) will also be emailed to attendees who miss
the meeting, so slide titles need to carry the argument on their own without narration.

## Raw material / sources

Two source documents contain everything needed for this deck. Do not introduce numbers, quotes,
or milestones that aren't traceable to one of these two files:

1. `sources/metrics-summary.md` — Q2 revenue, retention/churn, NPS, and shipped product
   milestones for Northlight Insights.
2. `sources/customer-feedback-notes.md` — verbatim notes from three customer interviews
   conducted in Q2, including direct quotes and named pain points.

If a claim can't be traced to one of these two files, cut it or flag it as needing verification
— do not invent supporting data.

## Key messages (3-4, in priority order)

1. **Growth reaccelerated this quarter on efficient spend** — expansion revenue and net
   retention both improved without a headcount increase (see metrics-summary.md).
2. **Customers are naming the product as the reason they expanded, not just the reason they
   stayed** — direct evidence from the interview notes, not just satisfaction scores.
3. **Two roadmap commitments shipped on time**, and both are already showing up in the
   retention and expansion numbers — the team delivers what it promises.
4. **The one thing still costing us deals is a known gap** (real-time alerting — see the
   interview notes), and it's the first thing Q3 budget goes toward — renewing the budget is
   what closes the gap the customers are already telling us about.

## Known objections to pre-empt

- "Haven't we heard 'growth is reaccelerating' before?" — have the quarter-over-quarter
  comparison ready, not just this quarter's snapshot.
- "Why does infra spend need to grow if headcount didn't?" — be ready to separate the two line
  items; don't let them get conflated on one slide.

## Explicitly out of scope

- Next year's roadmap beyond the single Q3 commitment named above.
- Competitive positioning / market sizing — this is an internal renewal decision, not a
  fundraising narrative.
